Foundations of Cryptography

Explore the fundamental principles and rich history of cryptography, the science of secure communication.

Confidentiality

Keeping information secret from unauthorized parties.

Confidentiality is achieved through encryption, which transforms plaintext into ciphertext using a secret key. Only those with the correct key can decrypt the message and access its contents.

Integrity

Ensuring information hasn't been tampered with.

Cryptographic hash functions and digital signatures are used to verify the integrity of data, detecting any unauthorized modifications to the original message.

Authentication

Verifying the identity of communicating parties.

Digital signatures and message authentication codes (MACs) are cryptographic techniques used to prove the authenticity of a message's sender.

Non-repudiation

Preventing denial of previous commitments or actions.

Digital signatures not only authenticate the sender but also provide non-repudiation, making it difficult for the sender to deny having sent the message.
