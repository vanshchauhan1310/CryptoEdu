Practical Applications of Cryptography

Explore how cryptography is implemented in real-world scenarios to enhance security and privacy.

Secure Communication

Ensuring private and secure communication over networks.

HTTPS

Secures web browsing by encrypting data between the user's browser and the website.

HTTPS uses TLS (Transport Layer Security) to provide encryption, data integrity, and authentication.

End-to-End Encryption in Messaging

Ensures that only the sender and recipient can read the messages.

Apps like Signal and WhatsApp use end-to-end encryption, preventing interception by third parties.

Financial Transactions

Securing online payments and financial data.

Online Banking

Protects financial transactions and sensitive account information.

Banks use encryption and secure protocols to protect online banking sessions.

Cryptocurrencies

Digital currencies that rely on cryptography for security.

Bitcoin uses public-key cryptography to secure transactions.

Data Protection

Safeguarding sensitive information at rest and in transit.

Full Disk Encryption

Protects data stored on devices from unauthorized access.

Tools like BitLocker and FileVault use AES encryption for security.

Database Encryption

Secures sensitive data stored in databases.

Many database systems offer built-in encryption features.
